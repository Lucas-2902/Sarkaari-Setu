NCERT Website User Guide

Learn how to use the official NCERT website to access textbooks, educational resources, and academic materials.

📚 Step 1 — Visit the Official NCERT Website

Open the official NCERT website using your browser. The homepage provides quick access to textbooks, publications, educational initiatives, notifications, and important announcements.

Tip: Bookmark the website for easy access whenever you need study material.

📖 Step 2 — Access NCERT Textbooks

Navigate to the textbook section to download free PDF books for different classes and subjects.

  • Select your Class.
  • Choose the Subject.
  • Select the Book.
  • Download or read the PDF online.

🎓 Step 3 — Explore Learning Resources

NCERT offers various educational resources including:

  • Teacher Handbooks
  • Learning Outcomes
  • Educational Research
  • Training Materials
  • Reference Books
  • Question Banks (where available)

📢 Step 4 — Check the Latest Notices

Visit the Notices or Announcements section regularly for updates regarding:

  • New textbook releases
  • Corrections & revisions
  • Workshops
  • Educational programmes
  • Recruitment notifications

🔍 Step 5 — Use the Search Feature

If you cannot find a book or document, use the search bar to search by:

  • Book Name
  • Subject
  • Class
  • Publication
  • Keyword
Searching by the exact book title usually provides the quickest results.

⬇️ Step 6 — Download Educational Material

Most NCERT publications are available as PDF files.

  • Download textbooks.
  • Save PDFs for offline reading.
  • Print important chapters if needed.

💡 Helpful Tips

  • Always download books from the official NCERT website.
  • Check if a newer edition of your textbook has been released.
  • Use the latest edition recommended for your academic session.
  • Explore supplementary learning materials beyond textbooks.
  • Use the search function instead of browsing every menu.
  • Save frequently used books on your device for offline study.
Avoid downloading NCERT books from unofficial websites, as they may contain outdated or modified content.
