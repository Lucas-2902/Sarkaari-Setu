Property Tax Mutation User Guide

Learn how to apply for Property Tax Mutation, update ownership records after a property transfer, track your application, and download mutation records through your Municipal Corporation or State Government portal.

🌐 Step 1 — Visit the Official Portal

Open your Municipal Corporation or State Government's official Property Tax portal. Property Tax Mutation updates municipal records after ownership changes due to sale, gift, inheritance, partition, or other legally recognized transfers.

Tip: Property registration and property mutation are separate processes. After registering a property, you generally need to apply separately for mutation to update municipal or revenue records. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

📝 Step 2 — Fill the Mutation Application

Choose the Property Tax Mutation service and carefully complete the online application form.

  • Enter the Property ID or Assessment Number.
  • Provide details of the previous and new owner.
  • Select the reason for mutation.
  • Review all information before submission.

📄 Step 3 — Upload Required Documents

Keep scanned copies of the necessary documents ready before applying.

  • Registered Sale Deed, Gift Deed, Partition Deed, Will, or other applicable ownership document.
  • Latest Property Tax Receipt.
  • Identity Proof.
  • Address Proof.
  • Death Certificate (for inheritance cases, if applicable).
  • Other documents required by your local authority.
Document requirements may vary depending on your state or municipal authority. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

💳 Step 4 — Pay the Applicable Fee

If a mutation fee is applicable, complete the payment using the available online payment options.

  • Pay the prescribed mutation fee.
  • Save the payment receipt.
  • Keep the transaction reference for future use.

📋 Step 5 — Track Your Application

After submitting your application, use your Application Number or Acknowledgement Number to monitor its progress.

  • Track verification status.
  • Check for document requests.
  • Monitor approval updates.

📥 Step 6 — Download the Updated Record

Once your application is approved, download or view the updated property tax record showing the new owner's details.

  • Verify all ownership information.
  • Save a digital copy.
  • Print the updated record if required.

💡 Helpful Tips

  • Apply for mutation soon after the property transfer is completed.
  • Verify that all ownership details are correct before submission.
  • Keep your application number and payment receipt safe.
  • Ensure there are no pending property tax dues.
  • Download the updated record after approval.
  • Use only the official government or municipal portal.
Always apply through your official Municipal Corporation or State Government portal. Never share your OTP, payment details, or personal information with unauthorized websites or individuals.
