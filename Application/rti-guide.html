RTI Application Guide

Learn how to file an RTI (Right to Information) Application, submit the required details, pay the application fee, track your request, and obtain information from public authorities through the official RTI portal.

🌐 Step 1 — Visit the Official RTI Portal

Open the official RTI Online Portal or the concerned Public Authority's website. The portal allows citizens to submit RTI requests, make payments, track applications, and receive responses online.

Tip: RTI applications can be filed to seek information from government departments, ministries, public authorities, and certain government-funded organizations.

📝 Step 2 — Prepare Your RTI Request

Before submitting your application, clearly identify the information you wish to obtain.

  • Write specific questions.
  • Request existing records or documents.
  • Mention the relevant department if known.
  • Avoid asking for opinions or explanations.
  • Keep the request clear and concise.
Well-structured RTI applications are easier for public authorities to process and respond to.

📋 Step 3 — Submit the RTI Application

Select the Submit RTI Request service and complete the application form.

  • Enter applicant details.
  • Select the concerned public authority.
  • Provide the subject of the request.
  • Enter your RTI questions.
  • Review all information before submission.

💳 Step 4 — Pay the Application Fee

Complete the online payment using the available payment methods, if applicable.

  • Pay the prescribed RTI application fee.
  • Save the payment receipt.
  • Keep the transaction reference number.
Additional charges may apply if large volumes of documents or records are requested.

📋 Step 5 — Track Your Application

After submitting your RTI request, use the Registration Number or Reference Number to monitor its progress.

  • Track application status.
  • View responses from the public authority.
  • Check for additional fee requests.
  • Monitor updates online.

📥 Step 6 — Receive the Information

Once processed, the public authority may provide the requested information electronically, by post, or through other approved means.

  • Download available documents.
  • Review the response carefully.
  • Save copies for future reference.
  • File an appeal if applicable under RTI provisions.

💡 Helpful Tips

  • Ask clear and specific questions.
  • Request information that already exists in records.
  • Keep your RTI application concise.
  • Save your application and payment details.
  • Track your application regularly.
  • Use only the official RTI portal or authorized public authority website.
Do not include unnecessary personal information in your RTI request. Always use official government portals for filing RTI applications and making payments.
