Stray Animal Complaint Guide

Learn how to report stray animals, injured animals, animal nuisance, or animal-related public safety concerns through your Municipal Corporation or local government's official complaint portal.

🌐 Step 1 — Visit the Official Portal

Open your Municipal Corporation's official grievance portal or Animal Welfare section. Many local authorities allow citizens to report stray animal issues online and track the status of their complaints.

Tip: Report only genuine cases that require municipal or animal control assistance. If there is an immediate emergency involving a person or animal, contact your local emergency services.

📝 Step 2 — Register Your Complaint

Select the Stray Animal Complaint service and complete the online complaint form.

  • Enter your contact details (if required).
  • Provide the exact location of the animal.
  • Select the complaint category.
  • Describe the situation clearly.
  • Review all information before submitting.

📷 Step 3 — Upload Supporting Evidence

Attach clear photographs and location details to help the concerned department identify the animal and respond appropriately.

  • Photos of the animal (if safe to take).
  • Complete address or nearby landmark.
  • Additional comments about the situation.
  • Other supporting information, if available.
Only take photographs if it is safe to do so. Do not approach or disturb aggressive, frightened, or injured animals.

🐕 Step 4 — Common Stray Animal Complaints

Municipal authorities commonly accept complaints related to:

  • Stray dogs.
  • Stray cattle.
  • Injured stray animals.
  • Aggressive stray animals.
  • Animal nuisance in public places.
  • Other animal-related public safety concerns.

📋 Step 5 — Track Your Complaint

After submitting your complaint, use the Complaint Number or Reference Number to monitor its progress.

  • Track complaint status.
  • View inspection or response updates.
  • Receive notifications from the authority.
  • Check for requests for additional information.
Keep your Complaint Number safe until the complaint has been resolved.

💡 Helpful Tips

  • Provide the exact location of the animal.
  • Upload clear photographs whenever possible.
  • Do not attempt to capture or handle stray animals yourself.
  • Avoid submitting duplicate complaints.
  • Track your complaint regularly.
  • Use only the official Municipal Corporation portal.
Submit only genuine complaints with accurate information. Never provoke, harm, or attempt to relocate stray animals on your own. Follow the instructions of the concerned authorities.
