Road Repair Complaint Guide

Learn how to report damaged roads, potholes, broken pavements, or other road-related issues, upload supporting evidence, track your complaint, and receive updates through your Municipal Corporation, Public Works Department (PWD), or local government portal.

🌐 Step 1 — Visit the Official Portal

Open your Municipal Corporation's official grievance portal or the Public Works Department (PWD) website. Most local authorities provide online services for reporting road repair issues and tracking complaint status.

Tip: Report road damage as soon as possible to help improve public safety and prevent accidents.

📝 Step 2 — Register Your Complaint

Select the Road Repair Complaint service and carefully complete the complaint form.

  • Enter your contact details (if required).
  • Provide the exact location of the damaged road.
  • Select the complaint category.
  • Describe the issue clearly.
  • Review all information before submitting.

📷 Step 3 — Upload Supporting Evidence

Attach clear photographs and location details to help the concerned authority identify the problem quickly.

  • Photos of the damaged road or pothole.
  • Complete address or nearby landmark.
  • Road name (if known).
  • Additional comments or supporting information.
Providing accurate location details and clear photographs helps authorities inspect and repair the road more efficiently.

🛣️ Step 4 — Common Road Issues

You can report a variety of road-related problems, including:

  • Potholes.
  • Damaged or uneven road surface.
  • Broken footpaths or pavements.
  • Road cave-ins or surface cracks.
  • Damaged road dividers or curbs.
  • Other road maintenance issues.

📋 Step 5 — Track Your Complaint

After submitting your complaint, use the Complaint Number or Reference Number to monitor its progress.

  • Track complaint status.
  • View inspection updates.
  • Receive notifications from the authority.
  • Check for requests for additional information.
Keep your Complaint Number safe until the issue has been resolved.

💡 Helpful Tips

  • Provide the exact road location.
  • Upload clear photographs whenever possible.
  • Mention nearby landmarks to help locate the issue.
  • Avoid submitting duplicate complaints.
  • Track your complaint regularly.
  • Use only the official Municipal Corporation or PWD portal.
Submit only genuine complaints with accurate information. False or misleading complaints may delay maintenance work and affect public services.
