NTA Website User Guide

Learn how to use the official National Testing Agency (NTA) website for entrance examinations, application forms, admit cards, answer keys, and results.

🌐 Step 1 — Visit the Official NTA Website

Open the official NTA website. The homepage displays the latest public notices, examination announcements, important dates, and links to active examinations.

Tip: Check the homepage regularly during application and result periods for the latest updates.

📝 Step 2 — Select Your Examination

Choose the examination you want to access, such as:

  • JEE (Main)
  • NEET (UG)
  • CUET (UG/PG)
  • UGC NET
  • CMAT
  • GPAT
  • SWAYAM
  • Other NTA examinations

📋 Step 3 — Fill the Application Form

If applications are open, complete the online registration by entering accurate personal, academic, and contact details.

  • Create/Login to your account.
  • Fill the application form.
  • Upload the required documents.
  • Pay the application fee (if applicable).
  • Download the confirmation page after successful submission.

📄 Step 4 — Download Your Admit Card

Once admit cards are released, log in using your Application Number and Date of Birth or Password.

  • Download the admit card.
  • Verify your personal details.
  • Check the exam centre and reporting time.
  • Print a clear copy for exam day.
Carry all documents mentioned in the admit card instructions.

📢 Step 5 — Check Answer Keys & Results

After the examination, the NTA website publishes:

  • Provisional Answer Keys
  • Challenge Window (if available)
  • Final Answer Keys
  • Score Cards
  • Results

📥 Step 6 — Download Important Documents

Save copies of the following documents for future reference:

  • Application Form
  • Confirmation Page
  • Admit Card
  • Answer Key
  • Score Card
  • Result

💡 Helpful Tips

  • Complete your application well before the last date.
  • Use the same mobile number and email throughout the examination process.
  • Keep your Application Number safe.
  • Download your admit card as soon as it becomes available.
  • Read every public notice carefully before taking any action.
  • Regularly check the website for schedule changes or important announcements.
Never trust unofficial websites claiming to provide results, admit cards, or application forms. Always use the official NTA website.
