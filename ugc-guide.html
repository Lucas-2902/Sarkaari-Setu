🎓 How to Use the UGC (University Grants Commission) Portal

The University Grants Commission (UGC) is a statutory body of the Government of India responsible for maintaining standards of higher education and coordinating universities across the country. The official UGC portal provides important updates, notifications, scholarships, and regulatory information for students and institutions. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Step 1: Visit the Official UGC Website

Open your browser and go to the official UGC portal. This is where all notices, guidelines, and student services are published.

Step 2: Explore Main Sections

  • 📢 Notices & Announcements
  • 🎓 Student Corner (Scholarships, Fellowships)
  • 👨‍🏫 Faculty Corner
  • 🏫 List of Universities (HEIs)
  • 📜 Regulations & Guidelines
  • 📊 Digital Initiatives (ABC, NAD, etc.)

Step 3: Find Your Required Service

Depending on your need, choose the correct section:

  • Scholarships & Fellowships → Student Corner
  • University Approval/Status → HEI section
  • Exam related info (like UGC NET) → NTA portal links
  • Regulations & Policies → UGC Regulations page

Step 4: Read Notifications Carefully

Always check eligibility, deadlines, and official instructions before applying for any scheme or program.

Step 5: Apply or Download Forms

If applicable, download forms or apply through linked portals such as UGC-DEB, UGC NET, or scholarship portals.

Step 6: Track Updates Regularly

The UGC portal is frequently updated with new guidelines, circulars, and academic notices. Stay updated to avoid missing deadlines.

⚠️ Important Tips

  • Always verify information from the official UGC website.
  • Use only government-approved portals for applications.
  • Keep your documents ready in scanned format.
  • Check deadlines regularly for scholarships and exams.
