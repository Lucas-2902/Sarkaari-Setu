Illegal Construction Complaint Guide

Learn how to report unauthorized or illegal construction, upload supporting evidence, track your complaint, and receive updates through your Municipal Corporation, Development Authority, or local government portal.

🌐 Step 1 — Visit the Official Portal

Open your Municipal Corporation's official grievance portal or the Development Authority's complaint portal. Most local authorities provide online services for reporting unauthorized construction and tracking complaint status.

Tip: File a complaint only if you reasonably believe that construction is being carried out without the required approvals or in violation of applicable building regulations.

📝 Step 2 — Register Your Complaint

Select the Illegal Construction Complaint service and carefully complete the complaint form.

  • Enter your contact details (if required).
  • Provide the exact location of the construction.
  • Select the complaint category.
  • Describe the issue clearly.
  • Review all information before submitting.

📷 Step 3 — Upload Supporting Evidence

Attach photographs and any relevant information to help the concerned authority verify your complaint.

  • Photos of the construction site.
  • Complete address or nearby landmark.
  • Description of the suspected violation.
  • Other supporting documents (if available).
Clear photographs and an accurate location help authorities inspect the site more efficiently.

🏗️ Step 4 — Common Illegal Construction Issues

You can report issues such as:

  • Construction without required approvals.
  • Unauthorized building extensions.
  • Construction on public land.
  • Violation of approved building plans.
  • Unauthorized commercial use of residential property (where applicable).
  • Other suspected building regulation violations.

📋 Step 5 — Track Your Complaint

After submitting your complaint, use the Complaint Number or Reference Number to monitor its progress.

  • Track complaint status.
  • View inspection updates.
  • Receive notifications from the authority.
  • Check for requests for additional information.
Keep your Complaint Number safe until the complaint has been resolved.

💡 Helpful Tips

  • Provide the exact location of the construction.
  • Upload clear photographs whenever possible.
  • Include nearby landmarks to help locate the site.
  • Avoid submitting duplicate complaints.
  • Track your complaint regularly.
  • Use only the official Municipal Corporation or Development Authority portal.
Submit only genuine complaints with accurate information. Knowingly filing false or misleading complaints may result in action under applicable laws or local regulations.
