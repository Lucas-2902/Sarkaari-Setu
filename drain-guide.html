Drain Cleaning Complaint Guide

Learn how to report blocked drains, overflowing drains, waterlogging, or other drainage-related issues, upload supporting evidence, track your complaint, and receive updates through your Municipal Corporation or local government portal.

🌐 Step 1 — Visit the Official Portal

Open your Municipal Corporation's official grievance or citizen services portal. Most local authorities provide online services for reporting drainage issues and tracking complaint status.

Tip: Report blocked or overflowing drains promptly to help prevent waterlogging, foul odors, and public health risks.

📝 Step 2 — Register Your Complaint

Select the Drain Cleaning Complaint service and carefully complete the complaint form.

  • Enter your contact details (if required).
  • Provide the exact location of the drain.
  • Select the complaint category.
  • Describe the issue clearly.
  • Review all information before submitting.

📷 Step 3 — Upload Supporting Evidence

Attach clear photographs and location details to help the concerned department identify the issue quickly.

  • Photos of the blocked or overflowing drain.
  • Complete address or nearby landmark.
  • Additional comments (if required).
  • Other supporting information (if available).
Clear photographs and accurate location details help authorities inspect and clean the drain more efficiently.

🚰 Step 4 — Common Drainage Issues

You can report various drainage-related problems, including:

  • Blocked drains.
  • Overflowing drains.
  • Waterlogging due to poor drainage.
  • Accumulated waste inside drains.
  • Damaged drain covers or gratings.
  • Foul smell from drains.
  • Other drainage maintenance issues.

📋 Step 5 — Track Your Complaint

After submitting your complaint, use the Complaint Number or Reference Number to monitor its progress.

  • Track complaint status.
  • View inspection updates.
  • Receive notifications from the authority.
  • Check for requests for additional information.
Keep your Complaint Number safe until your complaint has been resolved.

💡 Helpful Tips

  • Provide the exact drain location.
  • Upload clear photographs whenever possible.
  • Mention nearby landmarks for easier identification.
  • Avoid submitting duplicate complaints.
  • Track your complaint regularly.
  • Use only the official Municipal Corporation portal.
Submit only genuine complaints with accurate information. False or misleading complaints may delay maintenance work and affect public services.
